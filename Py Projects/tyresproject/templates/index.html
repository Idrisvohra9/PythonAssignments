Tubeless tyres, in general, are considered superior to tube-type tyres due to their lighter build that also helps with fuel economy per liter. Also, unlike tube-type tyres, a tubeless car tyre or tubeless bike tyre does not have tubes that can get pinched between the tyre wall and the rim, reducing the possibilities of accidental punctures or PSI loss. Lastly, since the air is inside the tyre itself, there's less chance of instability at higher speeds as air distribution is more uniform.
